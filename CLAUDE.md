# Shivaji Maharaj — Children's Book Series

A middle-grade (ages 8–12) illustrated chapter-book series about the life of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. **Not a comic** — narrative prose with illustrations every 8–10 pages plus chapter-opener spot art (maps, fort diagrams, weapons, seals).

## Series structure
- **Volume 1 — The Boy of Shivneri** (1630–1656): childhood through the Javli campaign and the first stones of Pratapgad. **← current focus**
- **Volume 2 — The Mountain Lion** (1656–1666): Afzal Khan, Pavan Khind, Shaista Khan, Surat, Purandar, Agra escape.
- **Volume 3 — The Crowned King** (1666–1680): recapturing forts, Sinhagad/Tanaji, the navy, coronation at Raigad, statecraft, death and legacy.

## Targets per volume
- ~200 pages, ~30,000–40,000 words, 20 chapters.
- ~9 pages per chapter average (1,600–1,900 words text + 1 full-page illustration + 1 chapter-opener spot graphic). Set-piece chapters may run 10–11 pages; quiet chapters 7–8. Average must hold at ~9 to land 200 pages.
- ~16–20 pages reserved for front/back matter (title, map, glossary, "What We Know" notes, timeline).

## Voice & craft (see `style-guide.md` for the full rules)
- Straight third-person narrative, **dialogue interspersed** (~30–40% of each chapter), invented but anchored to documented attitudes.
- Light reading. Classic storytelling tropes throughout: **hook → beginning/middle/end**, **show don't tell**, callbacks and planted setups that pay off later.
- Each chapter ends with a small forward pull into the next.

## Sensitivity rules (non-negotiable)
- Anchor everything in **Swarajya, justice, and protection of ordinary people**. Keep the series about *character*, not community.
- Use Shivaji's documented orders protecting women, farmers, and places of worship of all faiths as primary-source grounding.
- **Violence**: keep the stakes and the moment, cut away from gore — tension before, consequence after. No glorified killing.
- **Legend vs history**: when a beat rests on legend (waghnakh details, some Jijabai stories, the Bijapur court visit, the Raireshwar oath wording), flag it in the per-chapter "What We Know" endnote. Dramatised dialogue is noted as dramatised.

## Workflow
- One file per chapter: `chapters/chXX.md`.
- **Draft Chapter 1 alone first** to lock voice, then batches of **2–3 chapters max per session** (quality ceiling — the back half goes flat if you push for more).
- Before drafting a chapter, read the relevant prior chapters for continuity (what characters have said, what's been planted, existing descriptions of places).
- After each chapter, update `continuity.md` (character voices, planted setups, place descriptions, timeline).
- Later phases (do NOT start until text is done): extract characters/locations/objects into `reference/` for illustration reference images, then build per-scene descriptions for key events.

## Files
- `outline.md` — Volume 1 beat sheet (20 chapters, the source of truth for what each covers).
- `style-guide.md` — voice, vocabulary ceiling, dialogue rules, Marathi-term handling.
- `continuity.md` — running notes updated after every chapter.
- `chapters/` — the drafts.
- `reference/` — character/location/object reference (later phase).
