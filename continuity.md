## Character voices & facts

- **Shivaji** — born Feb 1630, Shivneri fort. Curious, growing resolve. Narration POV anchor. *(Ch.1: newborn.)* **Ch.2 (age ~5):** all black hair and elbows, "eyes that took a thing apart and put it back together." Counts things (the army's fires); relentless plain-logic questions ("We have some. They have none. So."); deep empathy — gives his bhakri to a hungry refugee boy at the gate; plays soldier with a wooden sword. Has never met his father; listens to Shahaji's letters trying to hear a voice he's never heard. **Voice:** direct, literal, fearless, kind. **Look (ref `shivaji-boy`, age ~5-6):** small Maratha boy, thick black hair, bright dark eyes, simple cotton tunic + small dhoti, little waist sash, often barefoot. **Older look (ref `shivaji-youth`, age ~10-12, aged-up from `shivaji-boy` for continuity):** same face grown, leaner/taller, small white topi/turban, training talwar + round shield — use from Ch.6 on (Ch.7 band, etc.) until a teen/young-man ref is made (~Ch.9, the 1645 oath, age 15). **Ch.6 (age ~8-12, verified-representative):** dual education under Dadoji — arms (rides, sword, spear, wrestling with Maval fighters) AND administration (land records, fair fixed written taxes, settling disputes). Learns the Maval terrain + bonds with its people (sets up Ch.7 + fort warfare). Lesson absorbed: *"governing well is harder than fighting well."* **Ch.3 (age ~6):** calls Jijabai **"Aai."** Rapt at the Rama/Bhavani stories; keeps asking the question under all questions — *"Where is a king like that now?"* Organizes the fort children into a "guarding game" (*"nobody gets past me to the village — that's the rule" / "Because somebody has to"*). First true **rage at injustice** watching a village burn; declares *"Someone should make a kingdom where this doesn't happen."* → Jijabai: *"Remember that you said it."*
- **Jijabai** — mother. Steady, principled, brave; tells stories of Rama and Bhavani; believes kings exist to protect. **Voice (Ch.1):** plain, level, never pleading; gives quiet orders ("you do not soften it"); turns fear into resolve rather than showing it. Does not flinch where others can see. **Key line / thesis of the series:** *"A king should be a roof, killedar. Something the small people can stand under when the sky turns to fire. Not another storm."* **Look (ref `jijabai`):** maroon nine-yard nauvari saree, green-gold border, gold nath, head-shawl; early thirties; in Ch.1 expecting.
- **Shahaji** — father. Warrior in service of the Deccan sultanates; largely absent; duty-bound. **Voice (Ch.1, via memory):** jokes to cover seriousness ("Then you'll know I'm winning, and too busy to write"), then says the true thing plainly. **Look (ref `shahaji`, matched to his documented 1923-sketch likeness):** full rounded black beard + moustache; tall pointed Deccan-Maratha turban with an upward-curving feather plume (kalga); long jama/angarkha robe with a sash; pearl necklace; curved talwar with disc hilt; grey war-horse. **Ch.4 (1636, verified-only chapter — Shahaji does NOT appear in person; no reunion):** his long defence of the Nizamshahi ends in honourable defeat; he submits and takes **Bijapur service**, sent far south to the **Karnataka** (Bengaluru base). Keeps the **Pune jagir**, placing it under **Jijabai + Dadoji Konddev**. Shivaji grows up **apart** from him. Elder son Sambhaji was more with the father in the south (prose-only, kept light). *(The earlier draft's invented reunion/sword-gift/farewell were cut under the verified-events-only policy.)*
- **Mavala foot-soldiers (the Maval hillmen)** — Shivaji's future infantry; appear from Ch.1 as Jijabai's escort. **LOCKED look (ref `mavala-soldier`, signed off; conditioned on the ArtStation "Maratha Mavala" reference):** lean, proud, big-upturned-moustache hill-warriors. Headgear = **wide, flat, TWIN-PEAKED deep-maroon pheta** worn low (NOT tall/pointed, NOT saffron). Dress = **white** side-tied knee-length tunic (angarkha) + **white churidar** trousers + wide **orange** waist sash. Footwear = **enclosed mojari/juti shoes with upward-curled pointed toes** (not open sandals). Arms = **curved talwar** (disc hilt) in hand + a **small dark round buckler shield with four bosses/"buttons"** on the front. Light, not heavily armoured. **When several soldiers share a scene, give each a distinct face/age/build — the reference is the uniform, not one repeated man.** Use this for all Maratha foot-soldiers going forward.
- **Deccan sultanate soldiers (the "outside" armies)** — Bijapur/Ahmadnagar (and later Mughal) troops; the antagonist armies, kept as *individuals doing wrong*, never a people/faith. **Look (ref `deccan-sultanate-soldier`):** heavily equipped cavalry — Indo-Persian **mail-and-plate armour** with lobed steel chest plates over a bright silk coat, a steel **"top" helmet** (domed, small spike, sliding nasal bar, chainmail neck-aventail), curved talwar, round steel shield, riding boots, often a lance. Deliberately **heavy/armoured to contrast** the light white-clad Mavalas. Vary faces in crowds.
- **The killedar of Shivneri** — old grey-bearded fort-keeper (unnamed). Dry, courteous, loyal; "buried more friends than he could count." Promised Jijabai he'd always bring news unsoftened. Functions as the witness to her thesis. *(Ch.1 only so far; ref `killedar-shivneri`.)*

## Places (descriptions, so they stay consistent)
*(Add a locked description the first time each appears. Visual refs locked in `reference/index.json`.)*
- **Shivneri fort** *(locked Ch.1)* — massive black-basalt hill-fort rising sheer out of morning mist; green crown of trees on top, pale stone wall along the clifftop; birds wheeling at the cliffs. Famous **seven gates** = a "trap of gates," each set at an angle, spiked wooden doors, murder-holes above. Up top: barracks, grain stores, a great rock-cut water tank, and the small **temple of the goddess Shivai** among the trees. Looks "less like a fort men built than a thing the earth grew." (refs: `shivneri-fort`, `shivneri-gates`, `shivai-temple`)
- **Maval hill-country** *(locked Ch.1)* — brown-and-green folded hills rolling away "like a frozen sea," terraced fields, big Deccan sky, west of Pune. (ref `maval-hills`)
- **Maval hill-village** *(locked Ch.3)* — small humble cluster of mud-brick/rough-stone houses, thatch & stone-slab roofs, a well, thin cattle, terraced millet, a tiny shrine; ordinary and vulnerable. The everyman settlement the whole series exists to protect. (ref `maval-village`)
- **Raireshwar temple** *(locked Ch.9)* — ancient austere Hemadpanthi stone Shiva temple on a high windswept Sahyadri plateau; square stone pillars, a simple stone lingam, a single oil lamp, doorway onto the ranges. The deliberate opposite of the golden Bijapur court — where the oath of Swarajya is sworn. (ref `raireshwar-temple`)
- **Bijapur durbar (Adilshahi court)** *(locked Ch.8)* — opulent domed Deccan-Islamic audience hall: soaring scalloped arches, rich carpets, hanging lamps, incense, a fountain, a high canopied jewelled throne. The seat of sultanate splendour — and of the kneeling Shivaji rejects. (ref `bijapur-court`)
- **Pune** *(locked Ch.5)* — Shivaji's father's town in the hills, wrecked in the wars; *(legend)* "cursed" with a donkey-drawn plough + iron bar. Deliberately rebuilt/resettled under Jijabai + Dadoji from the late 1630s. Ruined look = ref `pune-ruined`; reborn = green fields, busy lanes, market.
- **Lal Mahal ("Red Mansion")** *(locked Ch.5)* — Shivaji's childhood home built in rebuilt Pune: warm red-brown two-storey Maratha mansion, carved wooden balconies/pillars, courtyard, veranda. His first real *home* (vs. a fort). (ref `lal-mahal`)
- **Torna fort** *(locked Ch.10)* — the **highest** fort in the Pune hill-country; old name **Prachandagad** ("the huge, fierce fort"). A vast tilted mountain of grey rock with a broken crown of wall along its top, clouds catching on it in the wet season. In Ch.10 (1646, pre-capture) it is shown **neglected**: sagging great gate, grass grown thick on the unwalked wall, a sun-faded near-white Bijapur flag, a garrison that should be ~200 down to ~40 bored men who never look outward. A small vulnerable **village sits at its foot** (the everyman stake; ties to "a roof, not a storm"). The "tired proud giant nobody is watching" — chosen as his first target. **TAKEN Ch.11** (near-bloodless night assault up the unwatched northern shoulder; his flag raised at dawn; walls found crumbling/undefended). *(Later he builds the new fort Rajgad on a nearby hill, Ch.12.)*
- **Rajgad** *(locked Ch.12)* — Shivaji's own first true stronghold and **capital for ~26 years**, built up from the hill formerly called **Murumbdev**, paid for (per the chronicles) with the Torna treasure. A vast sprawling grey-basalt Sahyadri fort: a high central rocky citadel (the **balekilla**) on the summit, with long fortified rampart-walls and bastions radiating out along three broad plateau-spurs/ridges (machis). Grander and more deliberately built than tired old Torna — "a fort built from the first stone his way." Renamed Rajgad = "the King of Forts." (ref `rajgad-fort`)
- **Kondhana fort (later Sinhagad)** *(locked Ch.13)* — a mighty intact Sahyadri hill-fort ~SW of Pune (NOT a weak/neglected one like Torna was): a high three-pronged plateau, sheer black-basalt cliffs (~40m) rising to strong ramparts studded with regularly spaced watchtowers/bastions, two great iron-banded thick wooden gates (Kalyan Darwaza & Pune Darwaza) with dog-leg/twisting gateways, water cisterns, green in monsoon. Won by persuading killedar Siddi Amber, undamaged. *(Famous Tanaji night-battle to RE-take it = 1670, Vol 3 — do NOT use here.)* (ref `kondhana-fort`)
- **Purandar fort** *(locked Ch.13)* — a mighty twin/double-crowned Sahyadri fort SE of Pune: a high upper citadel (Balekilla) riding above a broad lower walled terrace (Machi) with several (five) large bastions and an imposing twin-towered gate, plus a smaller sister-fort, **Vajragad**, standing guard on the eastern side. Panoramic, strongly fortified. Held four generations by the Nilkanthrao family; won via the sons' quarrel. (ref `purandar-fort`)
